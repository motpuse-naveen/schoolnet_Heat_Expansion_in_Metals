Aim: To verify that metals expand on
heating.

Apparatus
Ring and Ball apparatus

Metal balls of same diameter but
different material, such as iron, copper,
brass, bronze etc.

Ring with a internal diameter slightly
larger than the diameter of the metal
balls

Bunsen burner

Procedure
Select a metal ball and attach it to the
chain of the 'ring & ball' apparatus.

Move the ball through the ring to check
that it snugly passes through the ring.

Heat the ball using a Bunsen burner.

Start the timer when you start heating
the metal ball.

Periodically check whether the ball
passes through the ring.

Note the time when the ball has
expanded more than the ring and will
not pass through it.

Stop heating by switching off the
Bunsen burner.

Allow the ball to cool and while it cools,
periodically check if the ball passes
through the ring.

Repeat the experiment with a ball of
different metal and note down the time
for expansion greater than the ring
diameter.

Select the metal ball to be heated by
clicking it from the options given below. Prior to heating, you can replace the selected ball by clicking on the alternative.

Heat the metal ball using the Bunsen burner. Click the Bunsen burner to switch it on.


As the ball is being heated, it will start expanding. Test the expansion by passing the ring over the ball. Move the ring up or down by clicking the handle of the ring and keeping the mouse button pressed.

As the metal ball expands, the ring will stop passing over it. When this happens, note the time and switch off the Bunsen burner by clicking on it.

As the metal ball cools, it will start contracting. Again check if the ring can pass over the ball by moving the ring handle up and down.

Click RESET and repeat the experiment with a different ball.
